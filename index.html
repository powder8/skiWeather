Snowpack Summary

True north aspects in the alpine remain the only areas holding dry snow with moist conditions at treeline and wet snow below. The moist snow will likely form a supportive crust by Tuesday morning at all elevations on solar aspects and between 2000 and 2500 m on polar aspects.Below the surface crust, the snowpack consists of 10 to 20 cm of moist snow over a series of melt-freeze crusts. The melt-freeze crust from mid-March is widespread, found 10 to 80 cm below the surface depending on elevation and location.The lower snowpack is strong and well-bonded.
